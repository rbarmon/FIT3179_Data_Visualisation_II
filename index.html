Japanese Prefecture Happiness Analysis

In 2020, a Happiness Ranking survey was conducted in Japan. This visualisation explores the aspects that could contribute to the Happiness Rankings in order to see what can be improved to increase Happiness.

Japanese Prefecture Happiness Ranking 1 ~ 47 in 2020

The Happiness Ranking survey presents that prefectures such as Fukui (1), Toyama (2), Tokyo (3), Ishikawa (4) and Nagano (5) have the top five happiness rankings, while the prefectures Kochi (47), Osaka (46), Okinawa (45), Aomori (44) and Nagasaki (43) have the lowest five happiness rankings.

Top 5 Prefectures for Crime Rates per 100,000 people

In this bar chart it can be observed that Osaka (46) has the highest crime rate per 100,000 people.

This fact could connect to Osakas low Hapiness Ranking.

Welfare Support Rate

In this Map it can be be that the Welfare Support Rate is quite high for prefectures with low Happiness Rankings such as Kochi (47), Osaka (46), Okinawa (45), Aomori (44) and Nagasaki (43)


However it may not be the strongest factor that contributes to happiness but perhaps a side factor.

Annual Average Temperature

It can be see in this idiom that the for prefectures that face more extreme temperatures on both ends but primarily on the hotter tend to have lower Happiness Ranking.

The hotter annual average temperature is depicted in orange and the coolor temperature in blue.

Typhoon Hits from 1951 ~ 2022

From this idiom it can be seen that the prefectures with a lower happiness ranking also experience more Typhoon hits over the past 70 years.

This trend could be related to the hotter weather experienced in these prefectures.

Japanese Prefectures and Regions

In Japan there are 47 prefectures and 9 regions. These regions are depicted in the following visualsation and are relevant in interpretting the following visualisation.

Prefecturewise Income Overview: Average Income, Minimum Wage, Welfare Rate, and Region

This bubble plot plots the Average Income, Minimum Wage, Welfare Rate to see where the key prefectures stand in terms of their financial wealth.

It can be seen that on average the prefectures with lower happiness rankings have lower average incomes and lower minimum wage while having relatively high number of people on welfare support.

Conclusion

While there are certainly multiple number of factors that contribute to a prefecture's Happiness ranking, the aspects viewed today present certain key characteristics found in prefectures that are less happy. These factors include loweer average income, greater Typhoon hits, hotter weather, and higher crime rates. Improvements in these aspects could potentially lead to improving the Happiness ranking of the prefecture.
